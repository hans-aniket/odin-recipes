Palak Paneer

Description

Palak Paneer is a quintessential dish in North Indian cuisine, celebrated for its rich, creamy texture and vibrant green color. The dish consists of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) simmered in a smooth, mildly spiced spinach gravy. The spinach is blanched and pureed, then cooked with onions, tomatoes, garlic, ginger, and a blend of aromatic spices such as cumin, coriander, and garam masala. The paneer cubes are often lightly fried before being added to the gravy, enhancing their flavor and texture. The combination of the earthy spinach and the mild, creamy paneer creates a harmonious balance, making it a favorite in many Indian households.

Palak Paneer is not only delicious but also highly nutritious. Spinach is rich in iron, vitamins, and antioxidants, while paneer provides a good source of protein and calcium. This dish is often served with a side of naan, roti, or rice, and garnished with a dollop of cream or a sprinkle of fenugreek leaves for added flavor. Its versatility and health benefits make it a popular choice for both everyday meals and special occasions. The vibrant green color and the rich, comforting taste of Palak Paneer make it a standout dish that showcases the best of Indian vegetarian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • cooking spray
  • ½ pound paneer, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons canola oil, divided
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • (10 ounce) package fresh spinach
  • 1 large tomato, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 2 dried red chile peppers, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh ginger root
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 pinch salt to taste
